Statement: There are studies showing common traits in the personalities of people according to the pets thay have. Hence we might even tell about the personality of residents of a particular state.

Dogs in States.

The above data represents the relation between States and number of dog owners. The size of circles is directly proportional to number of dog owner within the state.
Questions I can ask myself before starting a business in my state related to:
  1. Dog food and accessories.
  2. Dog's day care center.
  3. Supply of Dog's medicines.
  4. Dog's breeding center.
  5. As dog owners are mostly extroverts so I want to settle in such states or not?
Conclusions I can derive:
  1. States like Texas, California, Florida, New York are hot spots to start my business.
  2. I would not start my business in states like Utah, Nebraska etc.
  3. Many dogs within state means many dog parks.
  4. Many dogs within state means many dog breeding centers to buy a dog from.


Cats in States.

The above data represents the relation between States and number of cat owners. The size of circles is directly proportional to number of cat owners within the state.
Questions I can ask myself before starting a business in a state related to:
  1. Cat's food and accessories.
  2. Supply of Cat's medicines.
  3. Cat's breeding center.
  4. As cat owners are mostly introverts so I want to settle in such states or not?
Conclusions I can derive:
  1. States like California, Florida, New York, Ohio are hot spots to start my business.
  2. I would not start my business in states like Nevada, West Virginia etc.
  3. Many cats within state means many cat breeding centers to buy cats from.


Dogs vs Cats within States.

The above data represents the relation between mean number of cats and mean number of dogs within each state. The height of bar is directly proportional to mean of number cats and dogs within the state.
Questions we can ask from above data.
  1. Which pet food I want to keep a higher stock of?.
  2. Which pet accessory has a higher probabily to get sold out?
  3. Which veterinary store is more likely to work more?
  4. What is the personality of most of the people within the state?
Conclusions I can derive:
  1. I need a higher stock of cat food than dog food in many states.
  2. Cat accessories are to be sold out more than dog accessories.
  3. Arizona and Kentucky should have almost same amount of supply.
